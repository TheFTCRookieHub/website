Phase 3: Competition Prep

Weeks 9-12: Drive Practice, Judging, and Scouting

It's Not About the Robot, It's the Driver

A mediocre robot with an excellent driver will beat an excellent robot with a mediocre driver. Aim for at least 10 hours of stick time before your first comp.

Drill Menu

Solo Drill

The Ghost Cycle

Run a full teleop match (2 mins) alone. Count your points. Do this 5 times in a row. If your score varies by more than 10%, your robot (or driver) isn't consistent yet.

Chaos Drill

Obstacle Course

Scatter random parts/boxes on the field. Drive your cycles while dodging them. This simulates defense and crowded fields.

The 5-Minute Pitch

You have 5 minutes to tell your team's story. Don't wing it. Write a script.

Basic Script Outline

(0:00-0:30) Intro: Who you are, team mission, and "The Hook" (something unique). (0:30-2:00) The Robot: Focus on the "Why" not just the "What". "We chose a claw intake BECAUSE it was more reliable than wheels..." (2:00-3:00) Programming/Control: Mention sensors, autonomous paths, and unique code features. (3:00-4:00) Outreach/Connect: How did you help your community? Who did you learn from? (4:00-5:00) Conclusion: Lessons learned, future goals, and Thank You.

Tip: Rehearse until you don't need note cards. If you forget a line, keep going.

Scouting Wins Tournaments

You need to know who is good so you can pick them (or defend against them).

The "Paper Slip" Method

Don't overcomplicate it with an app for your first year. Print 40 small slips of paper with:

  • Team Number
  • Can they climb? (Yes/No)
  • Autonomous Score (approx.)
  • TeleOp Score (approx.)
  • Reliability (Did they break?)

Driver Selection Bootcamp

Don't pick the captain's best friend. Pick the best driver. Run these trials.

Trail 1: The Sprints

Drive from one corner to the opposite corner and back. 10 times.
Goal: Straight lines. No drifting. < 20 seconds.

Trial 2: The Precision Park

Place tape on the floor. Driver must stop with wheels exactly on the tape.
Goal: Control at low speeds.

Scouting Database Template

Set up a Google Sheet with these EXACT columns:

Mock Judging: The "Mean" Judge

Practice these tough questions. Don't let them stump you.

  • "I see you used a servo here. Calculate the torque required to lift this arm." (Math check)
  • "Who wrote this code? Can you explain line 45?" (Anti-plagiarism check)
  • "You mentioned outreach. How many people did you ACTUALLY impact?" (Impact verification)
  • "If you had $1000 more, what would you change?" (Vision check)

Mock Judging

Grab a parent or teacher who knows nothing about robots. Have them interview you.

Common Questions:

  • "What was your biggest failure?"
  • "Who did the programming?"
  • "How did you fund your team?"
  • "What is Gracious Professionalism to you?"
