Before the Season Starts

1. Building the Team

You need more than just coders. A successful FTC team operates like a small startup.

Who to recruit:

  • Builders: Kids who love LEGOs or taking things apart.
  • Coders: Anyone interested in logic, math, or computers.
  • Communicators: Essential for the Engineering Portfolio and judging.
  • Project Managers: Someone to keep everyone on track.

Target Size: 5-10 students is the sweet spot. Limit is 15.

2. The Money Talk

FTC isn't cheap, but it's manageable with planning. Here is a realistic "Start-Up" budget estimate:

Item Estimated Cost
National Registration (FIRST) ~$295
Control System (Hubs, Phones) ~$560
Robot Kit (REV/GoBilda) ~$600+
Field Elements (Partial) ~$450
Tools & Supplies ~$300
TOTAL ESTIMATE ~$2,205+

*Does not include travel, state registration fees, or laptops. Look for Rookie Grants from FIRST!

3. The Workspace

You don't need a high-tech lab. A garage, classroom, or basement works fine. Essentials:

  • Storage: Bins for parts. If you lose parts, you lose money.
  • Power: Dedicated outlets for charging batteries.
  • Internet: Reliable WiFi for research and coding.
  • Field Data: You need roughly 12x12ft for a full field, but 8x8ft works for half-field practice.
