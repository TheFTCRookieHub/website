Surviving Inspection

Robot Inspection is pass/fail. You cannot play until you pass.

Common Failures

  • Sizing: Robot starts outside the 18" cube.
  • Sharp Edges: Metal protruding that could cut someone.
  • Wiring: Battery obscured or loose wires.
  • Numbers: Team numbers not visible from opposite sides.

Pro-Tip: Get inspected as EARLY as possible. Queue up as soon as the pits open.

Alliance Strategy

When you are paired with another team, talk to them immediately.

The Pre-Match Huddle Checklist:

  • What is your autonomous? (Where do you start? Where do you park?)
  • What are your strengths? (Are you a scorer? Are you a defender?)
  • What happens if we disconnect? (Do you push us? Do we stay put?)

The Perfect Game Day Timeline

Chaos is the enemy. Stick to this schedule.

07:00 AM: Arrive. Check-in. Drop gear in Pit.
07:30 AM: Robot Inspection Queue. (Go IMMEDIATELY. Don't wait).
08:30 AM: Driver meeting. (Drivers only).
09:00 AM: Judging Interview. (The most important 10 mins).
10:00 AM: Qualification Matches Start. Scouting team deploys.
12:00 PM: Lunch. (Eat. Hydrate. Sit down).
04:00 PM: Alliance Selection. Have your "Pick List" ready.

Pit Organization Strategy

Your pit is 10x10ft. It will feel like 2x2ft.

The "Front" (Public)

This is where you talk to judges/scouts.
- Team Banner
- Engineering Portfolio (On a stand)
- Giveaways (Buttons/stickers)

The "Back" (Work)

This is where you fix the robot.
- Battery Charging Station (Corner)
- Tool chest (Under table)
- Spare Parts bucket

Rule #1: Keep the floor clear. Tripping hazards = Safety Violation.

Gracious Professionalism

Judges are watching everywhere. In the pits, in the stands, field-side.

DO: Cheer for opponents. Help others fix their robots. Say "Thank You" to volunteers.

DON'T: Yell at refs. Throw controllers. Blame your alliance partner.
